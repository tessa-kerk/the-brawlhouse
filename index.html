Behind the concept

Why this works

The game is the proof. Here's the thinking behind it — the gap it fills, the precedent it builds on, and what success would look like.

The problem

Engagement peaks for 72 hours, then falls back to baseline

Brawl Stars ships a major update every 5–6 weeks. Each one is a marketing moment — Brawl Talk drops, creators post, the community reacts. By week two it's back at baseline. Players get the update information; they just don't get to interact with it. Nothing bridges the gap between cycles.

72hPeak engagement after Brawl Talk
5–6 wksBetween major updates
$0Organic hook in the gap
The precedent

Supercell has already proven the instinct

For Brawl Stars' 5th anniversary, Supercell built an interactive Starr Park CCTV experience — hidden timecodes, unlockable story drops. It became the biggest organic engagement moment in the game's history. The BrawlHouse takes that same lore-discovery instinct and makes it conversational, and repeatable every season.

The target

Benchmarked against the CCTV moment — but built to run every season

A lower lift to build, designed to repeat rather than fire once. Targets set against the CCTV benchmark:
